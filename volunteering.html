Transcontinental Cyclist, Green Riders:Good Deeds on Bikes

Green Riders is a group of people who go on bicycle tours while aiming to have a positive impact on communities and the environment. I joined them in 2019 as we biked 2700+ miles across Europe from France to Romania. Along the way we planted fruit trees, picked up trash, and raised awareness about biking as a form of sustainable transportation. I like to include this experience because I feel it is unique and showcases my passion, dedication to reaching goals, and ability to go out of my comfort zone. I am still involved with this group and will be the official route planner for our next trip down the Pacific coast (once the pandemic is over).

Intern, UF Office of Sustainability

I worked with the Office of Sustainability under their sustainable transportation program to educate students, fix bicycles, and improve campus infrastructure. I enjoyed this role because it merged my passion for bikes and sustainability, and I was able to help the community through workshops and events focused on sustainable transportation. I also gained experience with GIS software through a project where we mapped the bike racks on campus. Some things that I really enjoyed were communicating with people on the topic of sustainability and teaching about bike safety and maintenance.

VP of Service Events, Gators for Environmental Community Outreach, UF

I served as the Vice President of Service Events for an Environmental Outreach club at UF. I was responsible for planning trash cleanups, organic farm volunteering, and other community service events. This was a valuable experience early on in my college career as I learned how to be a successful leader as an introvert while participating in conservation efforts in the Gainesville community.

Officer and Trip Leader, Outdoor Adventure and Recreation, UF

UF's outdoor club is near and dear to me, since I met most of my friends and have had so many amazing experiences with this club. I had the pleasure of being a club officer this past year, serving as the Gear Shed Manager, where I took inventory of our outdoor gear, discussed COVID guidelines with the other Officers, and purchased new gear for the club. I have also been a trip leader and have led 10+ trips, being responsible for logistics and group safety. Moreover, being a part of this club helped me grow as a person, expanding my horizons and challenging myself.
