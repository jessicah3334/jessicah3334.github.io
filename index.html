Jessica Hicks is an upcoming Environmental Engineering graduate from the University of Florida with a unique skillset in computational analysis and environmental leadership. She is a deep thinker and lifelong learner with a passion for communicating complex information and concepts to people of all backgrounds.

Jessica plans to start a career in data analysis, sustainability, and/or water resources. During her time in college, she gained experience with analyzing, visualizing, and managing computational carbon stock models through work with the Todd-Brown lab. Since this has fostered a love for coding, Jessica enjoys creating fun projects, such as this website, in her spare time. She is also involved with her university community, where she has held leadership positions with several environmental clubs and has been an intern with the UF Office of Sustainability. Jessica's deep passion for the environment often leads her outdoors, where she can be found riding bikes or climbing mountians. She aspires to foster a dynamic career where her talent can be put to use in a meaningful way.
